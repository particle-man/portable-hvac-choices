# Portable AC Decision Matrix — Dual-Hose Convertible Units (UK)

> **Last updated:** 2026-04-01
>
> **Scope:** Portable air conditioners purchasable in the UK with verified evidence of dual-hose conversion capability. All units listed are single-hose by default (the only type sold new in the UK) but have community-documented conversion paths.
>
> **Why dual-hose?** Single-hose portable ACs exhaust hot air outside through one hose, creating negative pressure that pulls warm outside air back in through gaps. A dual-hose conversion adds a dedicated intake that draws outside air to cool the condenser, eliminating negative pressure and improving cooling efficiency by up to 30%.

## Important Notes

- **Seasonal stock:** April is pre-season for portable AC in the UK. Stock levels are volatile — units that show "in stock" today may sell out quickly as summer approaches. Bookmark retailer pages and check frequently.
- **Warranty:** Modifying your AC unit with a dual-hose conversion will likely void the manufacturer's warranty. Proceed at your own risk.
- **Safety:** Do not leave a modified unit unattended. Sealing intake vents can alter airflow patterns and may affect the unit's thermal protection. Always ensure adequate ventilation around the unit.
- **Hose sizing:** Most conversions use 150mm (5.9"/6") flexible ducting, readily available from UK DIY stores and Amazon UK. Keep intake hose as short as possible (≤1.5m) for best results.
- **Noise values** represent manufacturer specifications. Actual perceived noise depends on room acoustics, fan speed, and operating mode. Sleep/night modes are significantly quieter on all models.
